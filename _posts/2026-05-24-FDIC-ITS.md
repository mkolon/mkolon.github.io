### Tuesday June 2 

- Here are a few details about the [Marquis data breach](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/marquis-ransomware-gang-stole-data-of-672-000-people-in-2025-cyberattack/) that's been mentioned a few times in class. Here are [some more](https://securityboulevard.com/2025/12/marquis-data-breach-exposes-hundreds-of-thousands-of-bank-customers/), and here are [even more](https://cyberguy.com/privacy/banking-tech-data-breach-exposes-672k-ransomware-attack/). For a quick exercise, have a look, discuss with your table, and see if you can find a place on the [Cyber Kill Chain](https://www.lockheedmartin.com/en-us/capabilities/cyber/cyber-kill-chain.html) that might have relevance in the analysis of the attack. Each table should be ready to (very briefly) discuss their idea.

- While they weren't exactly destroyed, AWS data centers in Bahrain [were struck](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/middle-east/iran-amazon-data-centers-warfare-b2950213.html) in the early phases of the current US-Iran war.

- I guess it's a good thing that the AWS architecture is [so insanely fault-tolerant](https://aws.amazon.com/about-aws/global-infrastructure/). It's a bit complicated, but I'd say almost anyone can understand it [if they have the right teacher](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/uJGwSLZifEg).

- Crowdstrike has a good primer on the [basics of Zero Trust Architectures](https://www.crowdstrike.com/en-us/cybersecurity-101/zero-trust-security/). If you're feeling super-human and you want more detail from a federal perspectective, [please be my guest](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/SpecialPublications/NIST.SP.800-207.pdf).

- Perhaps you're intrigued by the Cisco zero-day topic we touched on yesterday. I guess the good news is that [this little problem](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/over-10-000-cisco-devices-hacked-in-ios-xe-zero-day-attacks/) apparently only affects the Cisco OS version called IOS XE. 

- The FIDO alliance has [improved their site](https://fidoalliance.org/), which now does a decent job of [explaining how passkeys work](https://fidoalliance.org/fido2/).

- I mentioned the movie *Zero Days*, about the (alleged) Israel/US attempt to cripple Iran's nuclear weapons program using malware using the *Stuxnet* virus. [It's a great flick!](https://www.amazon.com/Zero-Days-Colonel-Gary-Brown/dp/B01I2C0UV6)

- When we talked about Environmental controls, I blanked on historic name for them. They're called [SCADA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SCADA) systems, and while they still exist, they have changed and been "consumerized" a lot in the last couple of decades.

- Garrett and I talked a bit about [Pegasus](https://www.pandasecurity.com/en/mediacenter/pegasus-spyware/) which will drive you crazy if you think too much about it. Who has to worry about this kind of stuff? There are many resouces on the internet about [how to detect and combat](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/blogs/what-is-pegasus-spyware-and-how-it-works/) programs like this.

- Back to passwords for a moment - [Hive Systems](https://www.hivesystems.com/blog/are-your-passwords-in-the-green) publishes a [nice matrix](https://class.hillvt.com/assets/passwords.png) of how long it would take current computing technology to crack passwords of different length and complexity. Now you see why we're *excited* about Q-day.

- I didn't even realize how old the [Mat Honan story](https://www.wired.com/2012/08/apple-amazon-mat-honan-hacking/) was, it's from 2012! [Here's another telling of his story](https://www.varonis.com/blog/lessons-learned-from-mat-honans-epic-hacking) with perhaps a slightly more even tone. It's important to note that the procedures used in this hack have changed considerably in the last 15 years, but holes in this cheese still line up sometimes...
